# How to Take Action

If Component Protection has affected you, here are concrete steps you can take right now.

---

## 1. File an FTC Complaint

The Federal Trade Commission has identified software-based parts locking as an anticompetitive practice. Your complaint adds to the public record they use to justify enforcement action.

**File at:** https://reportfraud.ftc.gov  
**Category:** Auto / Vehicle Repair  
**What to include:**
- Your vehicle year, make, and model
- The part that was locked
- The cost to unlock (or the cost of not being able to unlock)
- Whether a dealer refused to perform the unlock
- Whether CP triggered spontaneously

The more specific and documented, the better. Attach any invoices or dealer correspondence (redact your personal details if preferred).

---

## 2. Contact iFixit

iFixit is actively documenting parts pairing across industries and has engaged the FTC, Copyright Office, and Congress on repair restrictions. They want to hear from vehicle owners specifically.

**Submit your story:** https://www.ifixit.com/News  
**Email advocacy team:** advocacy@ifixit.com  

Tell them: what vehicle, what part, what happened, what it cost, and whether GEKO server issues were involved.

---

## 3. Contact the Repair Association

The Repair Association (repair.org) leads state and federal right-to-repair legislative efforts and is directly working on model legislation that would ban parts pairing.

**Website:** https://www.repair.org  
**Contact form:** https://www.repair.org/contact  

They are particularly interested in cases where:
- Server unavailability made CP removal impossible
- Dealers refused to perform CP removal on externally sourced parts
- CP activated spontaneously with no parts change

---

## 4. Contact your federal representatives

The REPAIR Act (H.R. 906) would require manufacturers to provide independent repairers access to the same diagnostic tools and data as authorized dealers — directly addressing Component Protection's dealer-exclusive unlock capability.

**Find your representatives:** https://www.congress.gov/members/find-your-member  

Sample message points:
- You purchased a replacement part for your own vehicle and cannot use it without paying a $300–$500 dealer fee
- VW's servers may be shut down, permanently bricking your vehicle's modules
- The REPAIR Act would fix this — please co-sponsor it

---

## 5. Contact your state representatives

Oregon and Colorado have already passed laws banning parts pairing (currently excluding motor vehicles). Your state may be considering similar legislation.

**Find state legislators:** https://openstates.org  

The Repair Association tracks active state legislation at https://www.repair.org/legislation

---

## 6. File a consumer protection complaint with your state AG

If you were charged for a CP removal that was described as necessary due to spontaneous activation (not from a parts swap), this may constitute an unfair or deceptive trade practice under your state's consumer protection law.

**Find your state AG:** https://www.naag.org/find-my-ag/

---

## 7. Document and share your story

Add your case to this repository using `cases/template.md`. Every documented case strengthens the advocacy record. Anonymous submissions are welcome.
